Hi, my name is Rahil. I am a storyteller who utilizes different mediums to tell original stories. Rahil's Archive, this website was created on June 1st 2020 initially containing only the timeline page. The purpose was to create something that would act as a central point for all the projects I had made over the years and since they occupy spaces on so many different platforms creating a singular place to access them all was a necessity. Over time this small concept got expanded into a blog, a journal and then I wanted to add a few new pages that would streamline and ease the goal that started it all - "To provide easy access to all my works".

The website you are currently seeing is version 2.0, it includes several new pages and some much needed aesthetic redesign. The full list of changes is given down below.

Version 1.0 (Original) - Remains deployed as it showcases progression.



Q1. What exactly is it that you do ?
People checking out my works might initially be confused due to the diverse range of my projects. I have composed music, created short films and video essays, developed video games, written books and also produced podcast episodes. I have been inspired by auteurs and polymaths, but since I have not achieved that level of experience yet, calling me by those terms would be a disservice. I personally define myself as a storyteller who utilizes different mediums to tell original and unique stories.


Q2. I am interested in your works, but where should I start from ?
Whatever piques your curiosity is a good place to start. You can check out the "Footprint" page for that. If you want a chronological timeline of my works from latest to oldest you can use the "Timeline" page for that. At any given point of time, clicking my logo on the home page will directly take you to my latest work.


Q3. Do you have any social media ?
No, I don't. The closest thing I have is my twitter profile. But I post like once a year there (currently not using it at all). So don't use that as a reliable resource for keeping up with my works. While not a social media, I do have an online journal where I record progress or share updates on at least a monthly basis.
